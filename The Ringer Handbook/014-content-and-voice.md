# Content & Voice

## Executive Summary

Words are part of the interface.

Every label, notification, button and empty state should reduce uncertainty and help a player take the next step.

Ringer should sound like a trusted teammate.

Never corporate.

Never childish.

Never overly enthusiastic.

Calm. Clear. Confident.

---

# Voice Principles

## Human

Write as one person speaking to another.

**Good**

"You're all set."

**Avoid**

"Your request has been successfully processed."

---

## Clear

Choose familiar words over technical language.

Use:

- Game
- Player
- Connection
- Join
- Host

Avoid:

- Event
- User
- Relationship
- Participate
- Submit

---

## Action First

Buttons should begin with verbs.

Good:

- Join Game
- Create Game
- Send Request
- Confirm Payment

Avoid:

- Continue
- Submit
- OK

---

## Calm

Never manufacture urgency.

Good:

"2 places remaining."

Avoid:

"Hurry! Don't miss out!"

---

# Microcopy Guidelines

## Empty States

Every empty state should:

1. Explain why.
2. Suggest the next step.
3. Keep a positive tone.

Example:

"No football games nearby tonight.

Try increasing your travel distance or create one yourself."

---

## Errors

Always explain:

- What happened.
- Why (if known).
- How to fix it.

Bad:

"Something went wrong."

Good:

"Payment couldn't be completed. Your card wasn't charged. Try again or choose another payment method."

---

## Success Messages

Keep them brief.

Examples:

- You're in.
- Game created.
- Payment confirmed.
- Connection request sent.

The interface should move forward instead of celebrating every action.

---

# Notifications

Only send notifications that increase the likelihood of someone playing.

Examples:

- A place has become available.
- Your organiser changed the venue.
- Three of your connections joined.

Never send notifications purely to increase engagement.

---

# Inclusive Language

Assume nothing.

Avoid:

- Guys
- He
- She

Prefer:

- Everyone
- Players
- They

---

# Writing Checklist

Before publishing copy ask:

- Is it understandable in under three seconds?
- Does it reduce uncertainty?
- Does it tell the player what happens next?
- Can unnecessary words be removed?

If yes, publish.

---

# Acceptance Criteria

- Consistent terminology across the product.
- Every CTA begins with an action.
- Error messages are actionable.
- Empty states always provide a next step.
- Notifications respect player attention.
